FAQs On Balcony Designs

Find detailed answers regarding layouts, materials, space optimization, and customization.

A balcony is like a small haven of nature in the middle of your house. It is a private outdoor space with plenty of potential to design it in a manner that suits your lifestyle and persona. So do you do this? Well, to guide you, here are some steps you can follow to create an ideal balcony space.

- Declutter your balcony and make room for everyone to move around freely.
- Bring in plants that are easy to maintain.
- If you have the space, add vegetable and flower plants to your balcony.
- Install space-saving seating arrangements such as folding patio chairs, seating with storage below etc.
- Add a turf grass flooring or tiles as per your home’s interior style.
- Use the wall space to hang planters and wall-mounted racks/shelves.
- Add proper lighting for evening hangouts in your balcony.
- Set up a safety grill around your balcony to prevent accidents and to keep your home secure.

The best balcony designs for Indian apartments combine style, comfort, and functionality. Popular options include balcony gardens with vertical planters, cosy seating corners, café-style setups, reading nooks, and multifunctional spaces with built-in storage. For compact apartments, space-saving furniture and smart layouts help maximise usability. The ideal design depends on your available space, lifestyle needs, and how you plan to use the balcony.

Balcony designs for flats and high-rise apartments should focus on maximising available space while maintaining comfort and functionality. Popular options include compact seating areas, balcony gardens, café-style setups, reading corners, and multifunctional spaces with built-in storage. Choosing weather-resistant materials and space-efficient furniture helps create a practical outdoor retreat that complements modern apartment living.

To make a small apartment balcony look bigger, use space-saving furniture, wall-mounted planters, and vertical storage solutions. Light colours, slim furniture profiles, and clutter-free layouts can also create a sense of openness. Mirrors, foldable seating, and strategic lighting further enhance the visual appeal while maximising functionality without overcrowding the space.

Few DIY tips to decorate a balcony:

- Add turf grass to the floor and some white pebbles on the corners to beautify the space.
- A hammock or swing to enjoy some me-time in your balcony.
- A foldable coffee table that is space-saving and functional.
- A textured wallpaper or a vertical garden on the accent wall to accentuate the beauty of the space.
- A wall-mounted bar unit with seating arrangement to host some cosy house parties.
- Some pendant lights or floor lamps to illuminate the space in the evening.
- A rattan sofa unit and a low lying coffee table with storage.
- A floor rug and some pillows to lend a comforting vibe.
- Some potted plants for a splash of green into your balcony.
- Blinders or roll-up curtains to protect you from harsh sunlight on a hot summer day.

The balcony is one of the most preferred areas of our home. It is the place where you love enjoying your morning cup of coffee as your watch the sunrise and set, take small breaks in between work or unwind with a glass of wine after a hard day of work. Colours play an essential role in elevating the look of your balcony. Here are a few options that work best for a balcony.

If you want to make your balcony look bright and vibrant, opt for a dark yellow. This colour will lift your mood and create a striking contrast against your green potted plants.Looking for some subtle colours that will create a soothing vibe? Opt for some simple off white or beige colour scheme for your balcony. They are lighter to the eye and make your balcony look spacious.

A blush pink or teal is for you if a chic and elegant style attracts you. These colours lend a soothing yet subtle fun element to your balcony.

If you want to lend an industrial look to your balcony choose some bold colours like grey and pair it up with a brick cladding accent wall. Be mindful of accessorising your balcony with proper lighting options to set the right tone for your evening parties.

However, be mindful of choosing a good quality paint for the exteriors of your balcony, one that is waterproof and heat resistant.

As most balconies have limited space you must choose your pieces of furniture wisely. Here are some suggestions for the same:

- Lightweight foldable chairs and table.
- Wicker swings.
- Wall-mounted tables and armless chairs.
- Light bamboo chairs and coffee table.
- Seating bench with storage to save space.
- Metal patio chairs and table.
- Wall mounted bookshelf with an attached bench for seating.
- Low-lying armless wooden sofa and coffee table.
- Single armless lounge chairs or ottomans.
- Rustic wooden sofa chairs.

No matter how small your balcony space is there are multiple ways to set up a balcony garden. Here are some smart DIY hacks to design your balcony garden at home.

- Use the balcony railing to hang plants in baskets.
- Go for vertical wall planters to stack some of your favourite flower pots.
- Use terracotta planters and arrange them along the boundary of your balcony.
- Bring in DIY planter boxes of wood/terracotta to create your small herb garden.
- Hang metal buckets with flowers in your balcony garden.
- You can also hang your flower pots from the balcony ceiling.
- Install wooden crates to grow tomatoes, chillies, herbs, etc.

The best flooring for a balcony should be durable, weather-resistant, and easy to maintain. Popular options include outdoor tiles, deck flooring, artificial grass, natural stone, and composite decking. The right choice depends on your balcony’s exposure to sunlight and rain, maintenance preferences, and overall design style. A well-chosen flooring material can enhance both the functionality and appearance of your outdoor space.

A child- and pet-friendly balcony should include secure railings, protective safety nets, non-slip flooring, and sturdy furniture without sharp edges. It’s also important to avoid toxic plants and ensure there are no climbable structures near railings. With thoughtful planning, you can create a balcony that is both safe and comfortable for every member of the family.
